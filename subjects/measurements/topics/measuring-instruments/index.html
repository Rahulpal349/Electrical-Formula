⚡ 2.2 Multirange Ammeter — Individual Shunts

Range Extension

Shunt Resistance

$$ \boxed{R_{sh} = \frac{R_m}{m - 1}} $$
$$ m = \frac{I}{I_m} $$

Shunt Current

$$ I_s = I - I_m = (m-1)I_m $$
$$ V_{sh} = I_s R_{sh} = I_m R_m $$

Multiple Ranges

$$ R_{sh_1} = \frac{R_m}{m_1-1} $$
Larger range → smaller Rsh

⚡ 2.3 Universal Shunt (Ayrton Shunt)

Safe Switching

Tapped Shunt

$$ R_1 = \frac{R_m}{m_1 - 1} $$
$$ R_2 = \frac{R_m + R_1}{m_2} - R_1 $$
$$ R_3 = \frac{R_m + R_1 + R_2}{m_3} $$

Voltage Equality

$$ I_m(R_m + R_{remainder}) = I_n \cdot R_{tap} $$
$$ m_i = \frac{I_i}{I_m} $$

Key Advantage

✓ No break in meter circuit
✓ Meter always protected
✗ Individual: switch gap → danger

📏 2.4 Voltmeter Multiplier (Series Resistance)

Voltage Range Extension

Multiplier

$$ \boxed{R_{se} = (m-1)R_m} $$
$$ m = \frac{V}{V_m} $$

Derivation

$$ I_{FSD} = \frac{V_m}{R_m} = \frac{V}{R_m + R_{se}} $$
$$ R_{total} = m \cdot R_m $$

Sensitivity

$$ S = \frac{1}{I_{FSD}} \;\; \Omega/\text{V} $$
$$ R_{total} = S \times V $$

🔵 2.5 Moving Iron (MI) Instrument

AC & DC

Deflecting Torque

$$ \boxed{T_d = \frac{1}{2}I^2\frac{dL}{d\theta}} $$
$$ T_c = k\theta $$

Deflection (Square Law)

$$ \boxed{\theta \propto I^2} $$
$$ \theta = \frac{1}{2k}\frac{dL}{d\theta} \cdot I^2 $$

Frequency Error

$$ I_m = \frac{V}{\sqrt{(R_m+R_s)^2+(\omega L_m)^2}} $$
$$ \boxed{C = 0.4\frac{L_m}{R_s^2}} $$

50 Hz

⚡ 2.6 Electrodynamometer Type Instrument

$$ \boxed{T_d = I_1 I_2 \frac{dM}{d\theta}} $$

As Wattmeter

$$ \boxed{\theta \propto VI\cos\phi = P} $$

Two-Wattmeter (3φ)

$$ P_{total} = W_1 + W_2 $$
$$ \tan\phi = \sqrt{3}\frac{W_1 - W_2}{W_1 + W_2} $$

🌡️ 2.9 Electrothermic Instruments

Hot Wire · Thermocouple · True RMS

Hot Wire

$$ H = I^2 R t $$
$$ \Delta\ell = \ell_0 \alpha \Delta T $$
$$ \theta \propto I^2 $$

Thermocouple

$$ E_{thermo} = \alpha_{AB}(T_H - T_C) $$
$$ T_H - T_C \propto I^2 R $$
$$ \boxed{\theta \propto I^2_{rms}} $$

True RMS

$$ I_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\int_0^T i^2\,dt} $$
$$ I_{rms} = \frac{I_m}{\sqrt{2}} = 0.707\,I_m $$

📏 2.11 Range Extension — Electrostatic Voltmeters

Resistance & Capacitance Dividers

Resistance Divider

$$ V_m = \frac{r}{R}\cdot V $$
$$ \boxed{m = \frac{R}{r}} $$
Power loss: $P = V^2/R$

Capacitance Divider

$$ \boxed{V_m = \frac{C_s}{C_s + C_v}\cdot V} $$
$$ \boxed{m = 1 + \frac{C_v}{C_s}} $$
Zero power loss (ideal)

Comparison

R-div: freq. dependent
C-div: freq. compensated
For higher m: Cs ≪ Cv
