MODULE 1 — Measurement Fundamentals

1.1 Accuracy, Precision & Error Types

Errors

Abs: $$ \delta A = A_{measured} - A_{true} $$
Rel: $$ \epsilon_r = \frac{\delta A}{A_{true}} \times 100\% $$

Accuracy & Precision

$$ \text{Acc} = 1 - \left|\frac{A_{m} - A_{t}}{A_{t}}\right| $$
$$ P = 1 - \frac{|x_n - \bar{x}|}{x_{max} - x_{min}} $$

Resolution

$$ \text{Res} = \frac{\text{FS}}{2^n} $$
$$ S = \frac{\Delta\theta}{\Delta Q} $$

1.2 Random Errors & Statistics

$$ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum(x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n-1}} $$
$$ \text{Probable Error} = 0.6745\sigma $$

Error Propagation

$$ \delta(A \pm B) = \delta A + \delta B $$
$$ \frac{\delta(AB)}{AB} = \frac{\delta A}{A} + \frac{\delta B}{B} $$

1.3 Calibration & Characteristics

$$ \text{Hysteresis} = \frac{O_{up} - O_{down}}{O_{FS}} \times 100\% $$
$$ \text{Dead Zone} = \text{Smallest input } (\Delta I) \text{ causing output} $$

Dynamic Response (2nd Order)

$$ \tau = \frac{1}{\omega_n \sqrt{1-\zeta^2}} $$
$$ \text{Rise Time} = \frac{1.8}{\omega_n} $$

MODULE 2 — Analog Indicating Instruments

2.1 Deflecting, Controlling & Damping Torques

General & Control

Eq: $$ T_d = T_c $$
Spring: $$ T_c = k\theta $$
Gravity: $$ T_c = Wl\sin\theta $$

Motion Equation

$$ J\frac{d^2\theta}{dt^2} + D\frac{d\theta}{dt} + k\theta = T_d $$

Damping Ratio

$$ \zeta = \frac{D}{2\sqrt{Jk}} $$
ζ<1 (Under), ζ=1 (Crit), ζ>1 (Over)

2.2 PMMC Instrument

Eq: $$ NBIA = k\theta $$
Lin: $$ \theta = \frac{G \cdot I}{k} \propto I $$
$S_v = \frac{G}{kR}$
$S_i = \frac{G}{k}$

2.3 Moving Iron (MI)

$$ T_d = \frac{I^2}{2} \frac{dL}{d\theta} $$
$$ \theta \propto I^2 \quad \text{(non-linear)} $$
$$ I_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{T}\int_0^T i^2 \, dt} $$

2.4 & 2.5 Dynamometer & Induction Instruments

Dynamometer (Wattmeter)

$$ T_d = I_1 I_2 \frac{dM}{d\theta} $$
$$ W \propto VI\cos\phi $$

Induction Type

$$ T_d = k\Phi_1 \Phi_2 \sin\alpha $$
$$ n \propto P = VI\cos\phi $$

Rectifier (Half vs Full)

$$ FF_{HW} = 1.57 $$
$$ FF_{FW} = 1.11 $$

MODULE 3 — Power & Energy

3.1 Single Phase Wattmeter Errors

CC before PC (Method A)

$$ P_m = P_L + I^2 R_{cc} $$
Error = $+I^2 R_{cc}$
Best for: Low R (High Current)

PC before CC (Method B)

$$ P_m = P_L + \frac{V^2}{R_{pc}} $$
Error = $+\frac{V^2}{R_{pc}}$
Best for: High R (Low Current)

3.2 Two Wattmeter Method

$$ W_1 = VI\cos(30^\circ - \phi) $$
$$ W_2 = VI\cos(30^\circ + \phi) $$
$$ \tan\phi = \sqrt{3} \frac{W_1 - W_2}{W_1 + W_2} $$
Normal lagging load mode.

3.3 Energy Meter (kWh)

Eq: $$ n \propto P \propto VI\cos\phi $$
$$ K_m = \frac{N \text{ revs}}{kWh} $$
Creep correction: Holes drilled in disc algorithm.

3.4 Power Factor Meters

Single Phase

$$ \cos\phi = \frac{P}{VI} $$

Three Phase

$$ \cos\phi = \frac{P_{total}}{\sqrt{3} V_L I_L} $$

MODULE 4 — Bridge Circuits

4.1 Wheatstone Bridge (Medium R)

Balance Condition

$$ P \cdot S = Q \cdot R $$
$$ R_x = S = \frac{Q \cdot R}{P} $$

Sensitivity

$$ S_b = \frac{e}{\delta R/R} $$
$$ e = \frac{E \cdot \delta R}{4R} \quad \text{(small unbalance)} $$
P = 100Ω, Q = 100Ω, R = 100Ω.

4.2 Kelvin's Double Bridge

$$ R_x = \frac{P}{Q} S + \frac{rp}{(p+q+r)} \left(\frac{P}{Q} - \frac{p}{q}\right) $$

Ideal Balance ($P/Q = p/q$)

$$ R_x = \frac{P}{Q} \cdot S $$
Eliminates lead/contact resistance.

4.3 Megger (Insulation Test)

$$ R_{IR} = \frac{V_{test}}{I_{leakage}} $$
$$ PI = \frac{R_{10 min}}{R_{1 min}} > 2 $$
$$ DAR = \frac{R_{60s}}{R_{30s}} $$

4.4 - 4.6 AC Bridges (Inductance & Capacitance)

Maxwell (Low Q < 10)

$$ L_x = R_2 R_3 C_4 $$
$$ R_x = \frac{R_2 R_3}{R_4} $$

Hay's (High Q > 10)

$$ L_x = \frac{R_2 R_3 C_1}{1 + \omega^2 C_1^2 R_1^2} $$
$$ Q = \frac{1}{\omega C_1 R_1} $$

Schering (Capacitance)

$$ C_x = \frac{C_3 R_4}{R_2} $$
$$ \tan\delta = \omega C_4 R_4 $$

MODULE 5 — Instrument Transformers

5.1 Current Transformer (CT)

Transformation

$$ \frac{I_1}{I_2} = \frac{N_2}{N_1} = n $$
$$ I_2 \approx 5A \text{ (std secondary)} $$

Errors

Ratio: $$ \frac{(K_n I_2 - I_1)}{I_1} \times 100\% $$
Phase ($\beta$): $$ \frac{I_m \cos\delta - I_c \sin\delta}{nI_2} $$

WARNING

$$ V_{OC} = N_2 \frac{d\Phi}{dt} $$
Never open secondary while energized!

Phase Error (β)

5.2 Potential Transformer (PT/VT)

Transformation

$$ \frac{V_1}{V_2} = \frac{N_1}{N_2} = n $$
$$ V_2 \approx 110V \text{ (std secondary)} $$

Errors

Ratio: $$ \frac{(K_n V_2 - V_1)}{V_1} \times 100\% $$
Phase ($\gamma$): $$ \frac{I_c X_1 - I_m R_1}{V_1} $$
Accuracy Classes: 0.1, 0.2, 0.5, 1, 3
NEVER short-circuit secondary.

6.2 Cathode Ray Oscilloscope (CRO)

General Measurements

$$ V_{pp} = \text{Divs} \times \text{V/div} $$
$$ T = \text{Divs} \times \text{Time/div} $$
$$ S = D/V \quad \text{(Sensitivity)} $$

Lissajous Phase / Freq

$$ \phi = \sin^{-1}(Y_0 / Y_{max}) $$
$$ \frac{f_y}{f_x} = \frac{\text{tangents (H)}}{\text{tangents (V)}} $$

Electrostatic Deflection

$$ D = \frac{L l V_d}{2 d V_a} $$
X-Y Mode renders parametric functions plotting $Vx(t)$ against $Vy(t)$.

MODULE 7 — Range Extension

7.1 Ammeter Shunt (Ring Shunt)

$$ R_{sh} = \frac{R_m}{m - 1} $$
$$ m = \frac{I}{I_m} = \text{Multiplying Factor} $$
Shunt placed in PARALLEL with meter.
R_sh = 0.111 Ω

7.2 Voltmeter Multiplier

$$ R_{se} = R_m (m - 1) $$
$$ m = \frac{V}{V_m} $$
Multiplier placed in SERIES with meter.
R_se = 9 kΩ

7.3 Instrument Loading Effect

Voltmeter Loading Error

$$ \%\text{Error} = \frac{R_{eq} - R_{true}}{R_{true}} \times 100\% $$
Meter R_v pulls internal current away from load.

Rule of Thumb

$$ R_V \gg R_L \quad \text{(Voltmeter)} $$
$$ R_A \ll R_L \quad \text{(Ammeter)} $$
